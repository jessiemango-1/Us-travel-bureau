🌎 US Travel Bureau - History

About the US Travel Bureau

The US Travel Bureau was a government organization during the Great Depression that promoted tourism and travel across the United States. Its goal was to help boost the economy by encouraging people to travel domestically, supporting local businesses and small towns.

It provided guides, maps, and resources for travelers and helped Americans discover destinations they might not have visited otherwise. The Bureau played a role in shaping early tourism infrastructure and promoting cultural exchange between states.

Fun Facts

  • During the Great Depression, travel was considered a luxury for most Americans.
  • The US Travel Bureau created brochures and guides to popularize scenic routes.
  • It encouraged road trips and the use of trains to explore the country.
  • Some of the maps and materials are now historical collectibles.
